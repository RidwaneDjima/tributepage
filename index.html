Muhammad Ali

A champion in the ring and a fighter for justice outside of it.

Here's a timeline of Muhammad Ali's life:

  • 1942 - Cassius Clay, Jr. is born to Odessa and Cassius Clay, Sr. at the Louisville General Hospital in South Louisville.
  • 1957 - Cassius struggles academically (he was later diagnosed with dyslexia) but becomes a school celebrity for his dedication to training, including running to school every day alongside the bus.
  • 1960 - Three months out of high school, Ali competes at the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome. He wins the light-heavyweight gold medal, defeating Zbigniew Pietrzykowski of Poland.
  • 1962 - Meets Malcolm X
  • 1964 - In his first heavyweight title bout, Ali is a longshot to defeat Liston, widely seen as unstoppable. Nonetheless, Ali predicts “a total eclipse of the Sonny”and wins by technical knockout in 6 rounds.
  • 1964 - Visiting Ghana, Nigeria, and Egypt, Ali inspires as a symbol of anti-colonialism. Welcomed in Ghana as a son of Africa and a hero, Ali reclaims his roots by saying “I am an African.”
  • 1967 - Refuses military draft in houston.
  • 1967 - Ali joins Martin Luther King, Jr. in Louisville at a rally for fair housing practices. The open housing demonstrations had protesters calling for a boycott of the Kentucky Derby on the same day, where members of the Ku Klux Klan offered to help keep order.
  • 1967 - Cleveland Summit in which leading athletes met with Ali to discuss his position on the draft.
  • 1970 - Ali VS. Jerry Quarry: First post-exile fight.
  • 1971 - Ali VS. Joe Frazier
  • 1971 - In a unanimous decision, the United States Supreme Court overturns Ali’s conviction for draft evasion, saying the Appeals Court had not properly established their grounds against Ali’s conscientious objector status. His passport is returned, and he is officially a free man.
  • 1972 - Makes first pilgrimage to Mecca-Hajj
  • 1974 - Ali VS. Joe Frazier (II) - Super Fight II
  • 1974 - Ali VS. George Foreman - Rumble in the Jungle
  • 1993 - Meets Nelson Mandela
  • 1993 - Recieves Presedential Medal of Freedom
  • 2013 - Inaugral Muhammad Ali Humanitarian Awards
  • 2016 - Muhammad Ali passes away
